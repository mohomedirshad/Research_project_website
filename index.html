About

A gemstone is a natural stone with a hardness of greater than or equal to 7 Mohs and was created as the consequence of geological processes. This current research is undertaken to create a system that can identify the gemstones of the Corundum Family. Accordingly, four characteristics can be used to identify the type of gemstone based on physical aspects. These four aspects are the refraction of light, the color, density, and the hardness of the mineral contained in the stone. Although Sri Lanka is naturally rich in the gemstone industry, the methods or techniques that are used to verify and validategemstones are yet manual and traditional. The method of verifying gemstones is fraught with challenges for both clients and traders. This research is conducted to invent an Internet of Things (IoT) based gemstone detector that can identify gemstones basedon their refractive index, color, and Cut-Shape, it is possible to effectively address the issues that customers and traders both confront. The refractive index, color, and Cut-Shape of the gemstone will be determined via image processing, and the IoT device will be utilized to cluster these properties. End users can view the summary of the results using the mobile app connected to our IoT device. Finally, this research study enhances the accuracy rate of gemstone identification in the Corundum family compared to the traditional methods used.

Project Scope


What we did to manage the project scope.

The project scope can be described using the Literature survey, research gap, research problems, research objectives, methodologies and technologies used.

1

Literature Survey

There are numerous gem families, and for this research project, the "Corundum family" has been selected as the gemstone family. The corundum family of jewels includes the following seven different varieties: ruby, Padmaraga, blue, yellow, pink, purple, and green sapphires. The reason for selecting the Corundum family is that it is highly valued and popular. There are number of devices to identify a gemstone such as: Loupe, Microscope, Refractometer, Lighting, Stands, Spectroscope, Polariscope, and Dichroscope. Gems' Refractive Index (RI) measurements will be given top consideration. The ideal tool for this is a refractometer, though it can also be done with a microscope. A refractometer can also determine a gemstone's birefringence and optic sign in addition to measuring the Refractive Index. In this study, an image of a gemstone will be used as input data for an image processing model that uses image recognition models and techniques, to identify the Refractive index value, color and the shape of a gemstone in the gem industry. If we consider identification, classification and analysis of gemstones, these are advance processes with a widely spread scope. Non-technical people are unable to conduct these tasks. Detecting gemstones need a specific environment like laboratory and requires skillful gemology experts, with advance tools, equipment, accessories.

2

Research Gap

When we compare the Research Paper "Portable low-cost open-source wireless spectrophotometer for fast and reliable measurements" with the proposed System it can have high accuracy. And, in this Research Paper they determine only whether the specific gemstone is Ruby, Sapphire or Emerald while in the proposed system we are going to determine the exact gemstone under the Corundum Family. In research paper "An Image Processing Technique for Color Detection and Distinguish Patterns with Similar Color" gemstones are detected using the gem refractometer which is used to detect refractive index and then image processing is used to classify images. In research paper "Development of a Gemstone Type Identification System Based on HSV Space Colour Using an Artificial Neural Network Back Propagation Algorithm" the image is solely detected based on color of the gemstone using image processing. In the research paper "Portable low-cost open-source wireless spectrophotometer for fast and reliable measurements" the gemstones are automatically detected based on color and cut of the gemstones.

3

Research Problems

One of the great challenges of gemology is the development of an accurate, simple, consistent, and reproducible technique for gemstone color measurements and specifications. At present people use their naked eye to detect the color of the gem. And in the gem laboratories they use color chart and compare the color of the gem with the chart and detect the color manually. This manual process of comparing can cause many human errors. gemology industry uses several mechanisms and technologies to analyze and detect gemstones depending various factors. But those procedures require advance tools, machines and accessories. Such as refractometer, spectroscope, dichroscope, polariscope, Mohs hardness scale and UV light torch. In addition to that it is compulsory to have expert knowledge, skills and years of experiences for analyzing gemstones. Due to unawareness of people about the cut-shape of gem and the unawareness of other factors which directly affects to the market value of gems, they can be easily deceived and it is possible to fraudulently sell synthetic gemstones to buyers.

Research Objectives

Refractive index of the stone. But we must use our naked eye to determine the Refractive Index value when we use the refractometer. The gemologists can easily read this value but itâ€™s too difficult to read this value to an ordinary person who is not in the gem industry. And, after reading this value gemologists use a printed refractive index chart to identify the gemstone for that refractive Index Reading. So, we are going to automate this by using Image Processing Technology and from the proposed system it automatically detects its Refractive Index and display the type of the gemstone. Color is an important factor for evaluating the quality of a gem. At present in Sri Lanka gemologists use printed color charts and naked eye to determine the color of the gem which can cause so many errors in detecting the exact color of the gemstone. To overcome these challenges, we are going to use Image processing technology along with Convolutional Neural Network to determine the color of the gemstone. At present ordinary people has no idea about the cut-shape of a gemstone by just viewing it. So from our research study we are going to display the cut of a gemstone by taking the image of the gemstone as an input using Image Processing Technology.

5

Methodology

First, we are going to gather data sets and, we are gathering knowledge from gemologists about the techniques they use at present to detect the color. Then we use Image recognition methods and gemstone image is used as input to an image processing system. The image processing results will be analyzed by performing image recognition. Then processed the dataset and created a CNN model using image processing for detect the cut-shape of gemstone. We trained the model with 500 images and tested with 200 images. After creating the model to detect the shape of gemstone, we took real time images of gemstones using capturing device on the IOT (Internet of Things) device as inputs for the model. To find the gem's refraction, a laser light will be utilized. The refracted ray will fall in a solid plane, such as a solid wooden or plain surface plane that is not transparent (so that it would not refract again), after the laser light strikes the gem and refracts in the gem. When the phone receives the refractive index, it will compare it to the published refractive index chart that is seeded to the database and identify the appropriate gem.
